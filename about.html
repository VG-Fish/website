About Us

Who are we?

The Student Led Chess Association, or SLCA, is a 501(c) nonprofit organization dedicated to helping the community through chess education. As a nationwide association of high school students, SLCA hosts numerous tournaments, chess camps, and other initiatives to give to the community, inspire change, and promote critical thinking skills. Our mission is simple: to improve the lives of young people through chess education. -run chess nonprofit organization.

Events

Given its status as an organization run entirely by high school students, SLCA has made remarkable achievements in promoting chess. Several statewide tournaments with over 100 participants have been organized and successfully carried out; in the summer, SLCA branches host C.A.S.T.L.E. chess camps to invigorate young minds in low-income communities, and numerous other events occur regularly throughout the year. For a list of some of our past, present, and future work, please consult our events page.

SLCA Alumni

The Student Led Chess Association (SLCA) is a non-profit organization that uses chess to improve local communities. SLCA is North America's largest student-run chess nonprofit organization.

SLCA Alumni

SLCA's mission is to promote chess and make a difference in communities. We offer a variety of chess programs and workshops at no cost. SLCA also hosts charity tournaments and helps teach unprivileged children. We run a high school league in several states and have 256 school branches

As a 501(c) nonprofit organization, SLCA runs entirely on donations, sponsorships, and grants, and members commit themselves to SLCA entirely for the sake of helping others out. Please consider donating to us if you wish to make a difference in the chess community.
